Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Welcome to our FAQ section! Here, we’ve answered some of the most common questions about baking cookies, storing them, and perfecting your recipes. If you don’t find what you’re looking for, feel free to contact us for more help!

1. What is the best type of flour to use for cookies?

All-purpose flour works great for most cookie recipes, but for softer cookies, you can try using cake flour.

2. Can I use salted butter instead of unsalted butter?

Yes, but you may want to reduce the added salt in your recipe to balance the flavor.

3. How can I make my cookies chewy?

To make cookies chewy, use more brown sugar than white sugar, and don’t overbake them.

4. How do I prevent my cookies from spreading too much?

Chill the cookie dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before baking to prevent spreading.

5. Can I freeze cookie dough?

Absolutely! Shape the dough into balls, freeze them on a tray, and then transfer to a freezer-safe bag. They’ll last for up to 3 months.

6. What’s the best way to store baked cookies?

Store baked cookies in an airtight container at room temperature. Add a slice of bread to the container to keep cookies soft.
